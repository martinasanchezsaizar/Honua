STAY

Great Southern Hotel: The Great Southern Hotel Sydney combines many charms of the old building with modern conveniences. With easy access to the city’s entertainment, shopping and tourist attractions. It is an ideal location for business and holiday travelers seeking comfort and convenience.

Sydney Boutique hotel combines elegant 16-room boutique accommodation with an excellent service. Every room has a private bathroom, air conditioning. Some of them have balconies facing Darlinghurst Rd and most of them have open windows.

Sydney Darling Habour: This Hotel is a nice option on a budget, you have many accommodations near, for example, the Darling Habour.

EAT

Xi'an Eatery, this is a Chinese restaurant that serves up bowls of thick, handmade noodles doused in chili and vinegar with meat and vegetables.

El Topo is just like a little slice of Mexico. You can eat Tacos and drink some Tequila and feel that you are in Mexico, and the menu is on a budget!

Fika Swedish Kitchen is a great place to eat healthy food in a very cool environment where Scandinavian and Swedish food is mixed.

MORE

Manly is uniquely placed, just across the harbor from the CBD it has all the cosmopolitan buzz of the city center and as the gateway to Sydney's Northern Beaches, it also offers a relaxed coastal vibe. It‘s a popular place to learn to surf, sign up for lessons at Manly Surf School.

Bondi Beach is world-renowned. The curved expanse of soft, white sand, the crashing waves, and sandstone cliffs all combine to create a spectacular seaside suburb.

Northern beaches: This stunning stretch of coastline spans a staggering 40km, starting at Manly and curving all the way up to the famous Palm Beach, a popular pilgrimage for fans of TV soap Home and Away. These coastlines include Narrabeen, Dee Why, and Avalon Beach.
