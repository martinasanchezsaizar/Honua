STAY

St Regis Hotel is extremely expensive but also a very luxurious hotel in midtown Manhattan. Guests often laud the hotel for toeing the perfect balance between Old-World beauty and charm, fused with a sleek contemporary outlook.

Park Hyatt New York: This luxury hotel is definitely located in one of the classier and trendier locations in New York, as Carnegie Hall is just down the street, and you take a short stroll to either Central Park or Times Square.

The Four Seasons Hotel is an unabashedly grand and extravagant hotel with high vaulted ceilings, marble floors and pillars, and over three hundred rooms and suites throughout its vast structure.

EAT

Eleven Madison Park: Swiss chef Daniel Humm mans the kitchen at this vast Art Deco jewel, which began life as a brasserie before evolving into one of the city’s most rarefied and progressive eateries.

Le Bernardin is the city’s original temple of Haute French seafood. Siblings Gilbert and Maguy Le Coze brought their Parisian eatery to Gotham in 1986, and the restaurant has maintained its reputation in the decades since.

Masa: Referred to as a Sushi Temple, Masa offers no menu, only sushi Omakase style. Put yourself in the hands of a sushi master to experience incredible slices of o-toro and tuna paired with white-truffle tempura.

MORE

The Metropolitan Museum is somewhere you must visit. Nothing says culture more than art, especially a place that houses centuries of art from all over the world.

New York City Ballet: Whether for an opera, a musical, or a ballet, an evening out at the gem-like Lincoln Center always makes for a wonderful, dressed-up night out.

Shop on the Luxuriest shops. Louis Vuitton, Yves Saint Laurent, Celine and many more are present on the 5th Avenue.
